# Comments & Interaction

KaraoGUI is interactive by design. Beyond scoring, a **live comment feed** keeps the
room engaged and gives the audience a voice — and a small way to earn points.

## The live comment feed

- Comments show up **live** as people post them.
- Each comment is **attached to the current performance.**
- The feed is **visible on the TV at all times**, so the whole room shares the same
  running commentary.

## Who can post and interact

- **Audience** members can **post new comments** and **interact with existing
  comments** (notably, give a **like**).
- Comments are a general engagement channel — participation here is open and
  encouraged.

## Likes

- Any comment can receive **likes.**
- A like is a lightweight way to say "this was funny/great."
- **Likes grant a very small amount of points to the comment's author** — see
  Scoring & Ranking (roughly 1 point per like, to be confirmed in the Technical
  docs). This is intentionally minor so that **performing remains the main way to
  earn points.**

## Ratings vs. comments (don't confuse them)

- A **Rating** is the audience's formal, scored input on a performance — **one per
  audience member per performance**, changeable until the performance locks in.
- A **comment** (and its likes) is separate: it's live commentary, not a rating, and
  its only scoring effect is the small like-based bonus to the comment's author.

## Visibility rules

Who sees the comment feed depends on the surface and the viewer's role:

- **On the TV:** comments are visible **at all times, to everyone** — including
  judges.
- **On phones:**
  - **Audience** members **see** the feed (so they can read, post, and like).
  - **Judges do NOT see comments on their phones.** This keeps the live commentary
    from influencing their evaluations. Judges can still glance at the TV.
  - Performers' phone experience is focused on performing; comment visibility for
    performers follows the same "on the TV for everyone" principle.

| Viewer (current role) | Sees comments on TV | Sees comments on phone |
|-----------------------|:-------------------:|:----------------------:|
| Audience              |         ✅          |          ✅            |
| Judge                 |         ✅          |          ❌            |
| Performer             |         ✅          |     (focused on act)   |

## Lifetime of a comment

- Comments belong to the **performance** they were posted on.
- They remain part of that performance's record; the **current** performance's
  comments are what dominate the live TV feed.
- Likes accumulated on a comment translate into the small author bonus per the
  scoring rules.

## Why this matters

The comment feed is the primary way the **audience stays active** rather than
passive. Combined with ratings, it ensures that **everyone in the room is doing
something** — performing, judging, rating, or riffing in the feed — which is the
central design goal of the game.
